Description: Many Techmarines upgrade their servitors to assist in battle 
as well as mechanical rites. Such models are easy to recognise, 
not just by their bonded weaponry, but also by the muscle 
grafts and hardened tissues used to make them capable of 
dealing and withstanding the sort of punishment faced in 
the duties of the Adeptus Astartes. Servitors that frequently 
accompany their Masters into combat are often a patchwork 
of replaced flesh, metal plating, and scars.
If a Techmarine requisitions a battle servitor for a Mission, 
he may also requisition weaponry to install in place of one 
or both of its servo-fists. This process requires no Tests if 
performed at a Watch Station with the proper facilities, and 
takes one hour per weapon.

What Q thinks is missing

Core Combat Mechanics
Not Yet Implemented:
    Swift Attack / Lightning Attack — multiple melee hits (like Semi/Full Auto for melee)
    Parry & Dodge reactions — opposed WS/AG tests to negate hits
    Defensive Stance — sacrifice attacks for defensive bonuses
    Overwatch — readied ranged attacks triggered by enemy movement
    Suppressive Fire — pin targets in an area, forcing Pinning tests
    Blast/Scatter mechanics — area damage with scatter direction (roll table exists but not wired)
    Grappling — opposed STR tests, actions while grappled
    Two-Weapon Fighting — attack with both weapons (talents exist, mechanics don't)
    Dual Strike / Whirlwind of Death — advanced multi-attack actions

Defensive Systems
    Force Fields (Iron Halo, Rosarius, Conversion Field) — percentage-based damage negation with overload
    Cover system — AP bonuses from terrain
    Pinning — WP test or lose actions when under fire

Character Mechanics
    Fate Points — spending (re-roll, +10, count as not dead) and burning (survive death)
    Characteristic advance XP costs — data exists in specialties but not wired to XP calculation
    Experience spending UI — buying advances, skills, talents from the sheet
    Renown & Requisition — mission requisition rolls, renown thresholds for gear access
    Demeanour mechanics — Chapter and Personal demeanour interaction (currently just text)

Cohesion (Partially Done)
    Cohesion Damage (Phase 3 planned) — 10+ damage from Accurate/Blast/Devastating triggers Cohesion loss; rally tests
    Cohesion Recovery (Phase 4 planned) — +1 from objectives, Fate Points, GM awards
    Fear-based Cohesion damage — Fear creatures causing Cohesion loss

Conditions & Status Effects
    Stunned — lose actions for rounds (Shocking button exists but no condition tracking)
    Prone / Knocked Down — movement and combat penalties
    On Fire — partially implemented (FireHelper exists), but no persistent condition auto-processing outside combat tracker
    Blinded / Deafened — sensory impairment penalties
    Fatigue effects — penalties at thresholds (fatigue ≥ TB = penalties, ≥ 2×TB = unconscious)
    Blood Loss — TG test each round or die when critically wounded
    Pinned — suppressive fire result

Horde Mechanics (Partially Done)
    Horde damage bonus — magnitude-based bonus dice to horde attacks (formula exists, not wired to attack rolls)
    Horde special actions — Overwhelm, Swarm
    Breaking hordes — morale/flee at low magnitude

Equipment & Gear
    Cybernetics — MIU, bionic limbs, augmetic senses (item type exists, no mechanical effects)
    Consumables / Drugs — combat drugs, stimm packs with temporary effects
    Grenades — thrown weapons with Blast, scatter
    Vehicles — no vehicle rules at all
    Requisition system — mission gear selection with renown gates

Campaign Systems
    Mission structure — primary/secondary/tertiary objectives
    Oath system — Kill-team oaths granting bonuses
    Distinction & Honors — earned titles
    Rank advancement UI — visual progression through Deathwatch ranks

Miscellaneous
    Skill tests — no general d100 skill test dialog (only combat rolls exist)
    Extended tests — multi-roll challenges
    Opposed tests — general opposed test framework (only psychic opposed exists)
    Interaction skills — Charm, Deceive, Intimidate, Command with DoS effects
    Jumping / Climbing / Swimming — AG/STR tests with movement rules
